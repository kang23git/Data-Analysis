Conclusions¶

  • The directors of movies are preferring to make movies with Drama as their genre. As 1753 movies have Drama as their genre, followed by Comedy, Thriller, Action and Adventure. This shows that the viewers must be preferring to watch the movies with these genres.
  • 2011 had the maximum movie releases that is 199 with 2013 and 2010 having 180 and 178 movie releases respectively. This shows that viewers had started showing more interest in movies from early 21st century. As the graph has always grown from then on till 2011. And, as I have calculated for top 20 years having maximum movie releases so, this shows that there is a decline from 2012 in number of movies released every year.
  • The average revenue earned by a movie helps in depicting the success of movie. As movies with high revenues were bound to like by viewers.

Limitations¶

  • There were many columns which had missing values in 1000's and they were removed beacuse they were categorical and I was not able to compute mean for those columns. I think they were not that necessary for analysis as they all were unique categorical values.
  • The results are limited to this sample dataset only as this is a really specific dataset for movies and the analysis are centric to the data columns of this dataset.
  • The analysis for revenue and budget for movies would be really strong and could have given results for movies profit and loss. The analysis of average revenue generated by movies in a year is also possible.
